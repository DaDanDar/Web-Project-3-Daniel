Treaty One Today

                        Today, opinions still vary regarding the many aspects and terms of Treaty One. Still, the Canadian government seeks to reconcile and acknowledges 
                    the wrongdoings of the past, choosing to lead a better future together along with the Indigenous people. In 2021, marked the 150th anniversary 
                    of the signing of Treaty One, with a ceremony held at Lower Fort Gary to commemorate this event. "The hardest thing is to work in partnerships sometimes ... 
                    you have to come to one mind and move forward and these are occasions to honour treaty relationships but also to hear the words that were heartfelt today 
                    with open hands and open hearts," said Canada's Indigenous Services Minister Marc Miller at the ceremony, "I'm hopeful."
                    
                    Source: Manitoba First Nations commemorate 150th anniversary of Treaty 1 signing - CBC News
                
                        There are seven Treaty One First Nation communities today: Brokenhead Ojibway Nation, Peguis First Nation, Roseau River Anishinabe First Nation, 
                    Sagkeeng First Nation, Sandy Bay Ojibway First Nation, Swan Lake First Nation, and Long Plain First Nation. Most of these First Nations today focus 
                    on economic development through urban reserves, which provide economic opportunities and support better self-sufficiency. Many urban reserves, like 
                    the Kapyong Barracks, are redevelopment by the seven treaty nations into a spot for business and community. These initiatives help generate revenue, 
                    create jobs for people, and overall enhances the community infrastructure. Many other projects, which includes the Crossing Gas Bar and commercial spaces
                    located on the Long Plain Reserve No.6, reflects the potential and growth of urban reserves to support sustainability. Such developments contribute to both the 
                    cultural aspects of these communities and the broader economy.
                    
                    Source: Naawi-Oodena officially becomes largest urban reserve in Canada after repatriation of Winnipeg barracks - CBC News
                    Source: Long Plain Reserve No.6 - Arrowhead Development Corporation
